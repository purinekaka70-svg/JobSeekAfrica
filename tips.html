Career Tips and Guides

Actionable advice for Kenyan students and new graduates.

Interview Tips: Tell a Strong Story

Prepare answers that show impact, not just duties.

Before the interview

  • Research the company and recent projects.
  • Prepare 3 stories using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).
  • Practice your introduction: who you are, what you study, and what you want.

During the interview

  • Listen carefully and answer with examples.
  • Show learning, teamwork, and problem-solving.
  • Ask smart questions about the role and growth.

After the interview

  • Send a short thank-you message within 24 hours.
  • Note what went well and what to improve next time.

CV Writing Tips: Keep It Simple and Strong

A clean one-page CV is enough for most students.

Structure

  • Start with your name, email, and phone number.
  • Focus on education, skills, and real projects.
  • Use bullet points with action verbs (Led, Built, Improved).

What to include

  • Relevant coursework or certifications.
  • Student leadership or club roles.
  • Quantify results: “Increased engagement by 30%”.

Common mistakes

  • Long paragraphs and tiny fonts.
  • Spelling mistakes (always proofread).
  • Listing every task without results.

Career Advice: Build a Real Advantage

Small weekly steps lead to big career growth.

Build a portfolio

  • Create 2–3 real projects and document them.
  • Share your work on LinkedIn or GitHub.

Network consistently

  • Reach out to alumni and ask for guidance.
  • Join student communities and attend events.

Keep learning

  • Pick one skill to improve each month.
  • Follow local industry leaders and trends.
